I work at IBM's TJ Watson Research Center in New York, where I study and develop algorithms for natural language processing. I got my PhD in 2016 from the Department of Computer Science at University of Colorado Boulder, advised by Professor Tammy Sumner.

Research

I am interested in computational analysis of natural language semantics. Problems that I have worked on in the past include identification of semantic similarity in short texts, question answering, automatic answer grading and domain adaption as well as multitask learning of these tasks. At IBM, my research is primarily focused on question answering. Most recently, I have been working on language generation models that can create synthetic training examples for QA.
